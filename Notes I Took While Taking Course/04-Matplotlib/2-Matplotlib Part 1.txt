Object-Oriented Method
# OBJECT-ORIENTED
fig = plt.figure()  # Creates a figure object (blank canvas)

# axes = fig.add_axes([Left, Bottom, Width, Height])  #axes. Here it means plural of "axis"
# Each number in [Left, Bottom, Width, Height] is a decimal representing the % percentage.
For example [0.2, 0.5, 0.4, 0.3]
# Left: Here = 0.2: Left Side Starts at the 20% of the canvas
# Bottom: Here = 0.5: Bottom of Graph/chart starts at the 50% from bottom of the canvas
# Width: #Width in % percentage relative to the canvas width
# Height: #Height in % percentage relative to the canvas height
# Note: These numbers have NOTHING to do with units of measurement on the sides of the axes.


axes = fig.add_axes([0.1, 0.1, 0.8, 0.8])
#axes. Here it means plural of "axis"
axes.plot(x,y)
# Now we put the plot we did earlier, but in an object-oriented method
axes.set_xlabel('X Label')
axes.set_ylabel('Y Label')
axes.set_title('Title')
